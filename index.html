Niki Lauda

"A hero and a Villian"

Niki in his Ferrari F1

Here's a timeline of Niki Lauda's life:

  • February 22, 1949: Niki Lauda was born in Vienna, Austria, to a wealthy family of industrialists.

  • 1968: Lauda started racing Minis and later moved to Formula Vee and Formula Three.

  • 1971: He joined the March Engineering Formula Two team.

  • 1972: He raced in 12 F1 events.

  • 1973: He joined the British Racing Motors team.

  • 1974: He joined the Scuderia Ferrari team and won his first F1 race.

  • 1975: He won five races to capture his first world championship.

  • August 1, 1976: He suffered a near-fatal crash at the German Grand Prix.

  • 1977: He won three races and finished in second place six times to win another world championship.

  • 1978: He joined the Brabham team.

  • September 1979: He retired from racing to focus on Lauda Air, the airline he founded.

  • 1982: He was offered a lucrative driver contract from the McLaren team and returned to racing.

  • 1984: He won his third career world championship.

  • 1985: He retired from racing for good.

  • 1993: He was inducted into the International Motorsports Hall of Fame.

  • 201: He was inducted by the Mercedes AMG Petronas F1 Team as a non-executive chairman.

  • May 20, 2019: He died at the age of 70.
